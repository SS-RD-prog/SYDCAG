The Proposal

What is SYDCAG?

SYDCAG — the Sydney Complex Aortic Group — is a proposed collaborative clinical programme between Vascular Surgery and Cardiothoracic Surgery at Royal North Shore Hospital.

Its purpose is to deliver structured, jointly governed care for patients with complex thoracic and thoracoabdominal aortic disease: cases that sit at the intersection of both specialties and that benefit most from simultaneous, coordinated expertise.

The premise is straightforward. Complex aortic disease does not respect the boundaries that have historically divided our specialties. At RNSH, the capability already exists within both departments. The gap is not expertise — it is structure.

◈
Joint Case Conference
Fortnightly MDT — all complex aortic cases reviewed jointly, decisions documented, outcomes tracked prospectively.
◉
Acute Aortic Response
Code Aorta protocol — single activation, both teams simultaneously, no ambiguity at 2am.
◎
Longitudinal Surveillance
Structured follow-up for post-dissection, post-TEVAR, and heritable aortopathy patients.
The Evidence

What the literature shows

0.31
Odds ratio for mortality in centres introducing a formal aortic programme vs their own pre-programme era
Mariscalco et al., Eur J Prev Cardiol, 2018 · 79,131 patients · 30 studies · observational
91%
MDT decision implementation rate over 5 years at the Barts Aortovascular MDT
Yates et al., AME Surg J, 2022 · 871 discussions · 649 patients
4×
Procedural volume increase at University of Washington following formal MAT implementation
Sorber et al., 2025 · single centre · 2018–2022
The 2022 ACC/AHA and 2024 EACTS/STS guidelines both document formal aortic team care as a defined standard — not an aspiration.
The Calgary Aortic Program — the closest international structural analogue to RNSH — restructured from a siloed two-hospital model in 2019. Referral volume grew from 54 to 221 patients per year within three years.
All studies cited are observational. Evidence is associational, not causal. The signal is nonetheless consistent and reproducible across independent institutions and countries.
The Next Step

The Founding Dinner

DATE
12
MAY 2026

The founding dinner is the first formal collective step for SYDCAG. Its objectives are not to finalise a governance document or agree a clinical protocol — those come later. Its objectives are:

  • Share the evidence framework
  • Hear from both departments — priorities, perspectives, and concerns
  • Identify participants for the post-dinner working group
  • Agree a start date for the fortnightly Joint Case Conference
  • Agree the next meeting date and scope of work
None of what SYDCAG requires to begin needs significant new resources. It needs agreement, structure, and a shared commitment from both departments.
