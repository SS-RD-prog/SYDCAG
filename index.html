Background

Why Now?

Complex aortic pathology requires coordinated input across vascular surgery, cardiothoracic surgery, interventional radiology, anaesthesia, and intensive care. The evidence base increasingly supports a structured multidisciplinary approach for both outcomes and institutional capability.

Analogue institution: The Calgary Aortic Program (Alberta Health Services) represents the closest structural model — a formalised, phased MDT programme built within an existing tertiary vascular and cardiothoracic centre.
🏥
Institutional Readiness

RNSH has the subspecialty footprint, case volume, and physical infrastructure to support a formalised aortic programme. The missing element is structured coordination — not capability.

📊
Evidence Base

Contemporary literature, including IRAD registry data and international consensus guidelines, consistently supports multidisciplinary aortic teams for improved decision-making and long-term surveillance adherence.

🔬
Research Platform

A formalised programme creates the infrastructure for prospective data collection, registry participation, and investigator-initiated or collaborative trials.

Programme Architecture

Three-Pillar Framework

SYDCAG is proposed around three interlocking pillars — each addressable independently but designed to function as a coherent system of care.

01
Multidisciplinary Aortic Team

A structured MDT meeting — initially monthly, then fortnightly — to review complex elective cases, discuss imaging, align operative planning across specialties, and ensure consensus-based decision-making for high-risk patients.

Monthly MDTCross-specialtyCase review
02
Acute Aortic Pathway

Standardised protocol for acute aortic syndromes — Type A and Type B dissection, intramural haematoma, penetrating aortic ulcer — covering activation, imaging, escalation criteria, ICU liaison, and operative/endovascular decision thresholds.

Type A/BIMH · PAUTEVAR criteria
03
Longitudinal Care & Registry

Structured follow-up — imaging surveillance timelines, medical optimisation, genetic screening referral pathways, and prospective data capture feeding a local registry and potential national linkage.

SurveillanceRegistryMedical Rx

Clinical Reference

Key Concepts & Thresholds

Reference material for discussion — including the evidence and controversy around current management thresholds in acute aortic disease.

The 14-day threshold: Current classification of Type B dissection as "acute" (<14 days) vs "subacute/chronic" (≥14 days) is a convention, not a validated therapeutic cutoff. The biological transition is continuous and does not reset at day 14.
